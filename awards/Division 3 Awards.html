    Division 3 Awards

    Division 3 awards at APA’s Annual Convention

    • Lifetime Achievement Award
      Division 3 honors an individual who has made long-lasting and distinguished theoretical and/or empirical contributions to basic research in experimental psychology.

    • Early Career Award
      Since 1995, Division 3 has presented awards to honor outstanding new investigators (i.e., no more than three years post-PhD).  

    APA Young Investigator Awards

    • Best Student Poster
      Division 3 presents this award to the top poster by a student at APA’s Annual Convention.

    • Journal of Experimental Psychology: Human Perception and Performance
      Award winner: Mike Richardson
      Richardson, M., Petrini, K., & Proulx, M. J. (2022). Orientation of tactile attention on the surface of the tongue. Journal of Experimental Psychology: Human Perception and Performance, 48(9), 926–942. https://doi.org/10.1037/xhp0001007

    • Journal of Experimental Psychology: Applied
      Award winner: Madalina Vlasceanu
      Vlasceanu, M., McMahon, C. E., Van Bavel, J. J., & Coman, A. (2023). Political and nonpolitical belief change elicits behavioral change. Journal of Experimental Psychology: Applied.

    • Journal of Experimental Psychology: Learning, Memory, & Cognition
      Award winner: Gesa Fee Komar, M. Sc. Heinrich-Heine-Universität Düsseldorf
      Komar, G. F., Mieth, L., Buchner, A., & Bell, R. (2023). Manipulations of richness of encoding do not modulate the animacy effect on memory. Journal of Experimental Psychology. Learning, Memory, and Cognition, 10.1037/xlm0001249. Advance online publication. https://doi.org/10.1037/xlm0001249

    • Journal of Experimental Psychology: General
      Award winner: Claudia Sehl
      Sehl, C. G., Friedman, O., & Denison, S. (2023). The social network: How people infer relationships from mutual connections. Journal of Experimental Psychology: General, 152(4), 925–934. https://doi.org/10.1037/xge0001330

    • Journal of Experimental Psychology: Animal Behavior Processes
      Award winner: Travis Todd — University of Vermont
      For the article: Todd, T. P. (2013). Mechanisms of renewal after the extinction of instrumental behavior. Journal of Experimental Psychology: Animal Behavior Processes, 39 (3), 193-207.

    Other awards presented by Division 3

    • Poster Award
      Division 3 presents this early career award at the Psychonomic Society's Annual Meeting, held online. To apply, email a PDF of the to be presented poster to SEPCS Awards Chair Audrey Parrish. Please include your abstract number.

    • Best Student Paper Award
      Division 3 recognizes the best student paper at the fall meeting of the Comparative Cognition Society. Future fall meetings will be announced on the Comparative Cognition Society website.

    • Women in Cognitive Science Travel and Networking Award
      Division 3 is pleased to honor the 2020 winners, Kara Moore, assistant professor at Oklahoma State University; and Gozde Mercan, assistant professor at the University of Toronto. To apply, please visit the Women in Cognitive Science (WICS) website.

    • APA Division 3 Award for Best 2013 Psychonomic Society Poster
      Julie Hughes (Rice University), for “Facilitation and Interference in Naming: A Consequence of the Same Learning Process?”

    • Best Early Career Presentation at the Fall Meeting of the Compative Cognition Society
      Audrey Parrish (Georgia State University), for “Chimpanzees (Pan troglodytes) Transfer Tokens with Social Partners to Accumulate Rewards in a Self-Control Task”

    • Lifetime Achievement Award
      Keith Rayner, Atkinson Family Professor of Psychology, University of California — San Diego
